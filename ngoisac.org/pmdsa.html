Digital Security Advisor (Program Lead)

Location: Remote USA Only; DC or Capitol area region preferred
Job Type: Full-Time (1-Year contract with possibility to extend)
Salary: $90,000-$100,000

About NGO-ISAC

NGO-ISAC is the leading cybersecurity community for non-governmental organizations, serving 400+ members including think tanks, human rights organizations, foundations, environmental groups, and healthcare nonprofits. It partners with private sector organizations both large and small. We're the cybersecurity backbone keeping civil society functioning in an increasingly digital world.

Our mission: Boost cybersecurity maturity in NGOs through strategic intelligence, best practices, and immersive education, fostering a resilient and collaborative security culture.

The Project

The NGO-ISAC is starting a new initiative to provide dedicated support to small collaborative cohorts of NGOs in demonstratively reducing their digital security risk together. This year-long program will support these collaborative cohorts with foundational digital security training, individualized guidance and support, and in-depth facilitated implementation efforts that will produce reusable tools, documentation, and/or procedures that address critical shared risks among cohort members.

The Role

This is a fixed-term, full-time position for one year with a salary in the range of $90,000-$100,000, depending on experience. This position will report to the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).

We are looking for a digital security advisor to act as BOTH a project manager and subject matter expert taking primary ownership over this project. All work will be supported by and in coordination with the CISO and other members of the security team. The ideal candidate is a self-starter with strong organizational and communication skills, able to work independently and collaboratively to advance NGO-ISAC's mission.

What You'll Do

Capacity Building Design & Facilitation

  • Develop digital security capacity improvement plans for cohort members to advance their understanding, preparedness and abilities to sustainably improve security practices.
  • Design and facilitate tailored, collaborative implementation projects for cohort members and participating NGO-ISAC members.
  • Coordinate with team members to ensure cohort needs are met from intake through implementation and follow-up.
  • Organize and facilitate in-person meetings.

Guidance and Consultation

  • Assess the capacity, priorities, and security needs of cohort members and translate them into specific, actionable organizational guidance and support.
  • Provide ongoing mentoring and support, through both formal and informal channels, to help organizations apply skills gained from the capacity-building program.
  • Monitor developments in NGO safety and security, identify key knowledge gaps, and collaborate with NGO-ISAC staff to address them through cohort-specific and broader NGO-ISAC content.
  • Help cohort members coordinate and share best practices and make effective use of the NGO-ISAC community.

Training Design & Delivery

  • Coordinate and/or deliver individual and small-group digital security training covering fundamental cybersecurity principles, practices, and tools.
  • Develop, review, and update training designs and materials.
  • Keep training content current and aligned with research and best practices.
  • Coordinate with the security team and other relevant stakeholders to ensure training meets NGO-ISAC goals.

Feedback & Continuous Improvement

  • Work with the CISO to design and refine digital security capacity assessment tools.
  • Collect and synthesize cohort feedback and other relevant data to assess program efficacy and success.
  • Use assessment data and feedback to evaluate program impact, identify gaps, and recommend improvements.
  • Document insights and propose enhancements for future program iterations.

Knowledge Production

  • Create, update, and standardize resources and processes, including training materials, so they’re practical and reusable.
  • Reuse or adapt cohort-developed documents and tools to enhance the broader membership experience.
  • Contribute to security awareness initiatives by providing expert insights on current cyber threats and trends.

MISC

  • Deliver outputs on time and in line with the agreed strategy, contractual requirements, and priorities of the CISO.
  • Represent NGO-ISAC at external cybersecurity communities and forums to:
    • Gather and share threat intelligence
    • Share, record, and reuse best practices.
  • Perform other related duties as assigned.

What you WON'T do

This role is not a technical support or consulting service for fixing immediate IT issues or augmenting existing staff of participating organizations. While the Digital Security Advisor may provide on-demand assistance for specific requests, the primary focus is on building internal organizational capacity rather than creating dependency.

Major incident response leadership for participants is also outside the scope of this role. The Digital Security Advisor may offer coaching during cybersecurity incidents, but organizations are expected to manage incident response through internal resources or specialized incident response firms.
